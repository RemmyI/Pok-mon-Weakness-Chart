The battle proceeds in a turn-based fashion, with each player taking turns to make decisions for their Pokémon.


Each Pokémon can perform different moves, which can include offensive attacks, defensive maneuvers, status-altering effects, or healing abilities. Moves have varying types (e.g., Fire, Water, Electric) and power levels.


Pokémon types play a crucial role in battles. Each Pokémon has one or two types, and certain types are strong or weak against others. For example, a Water-type move is effective against Fire-types but weak against Grass-types. Understanding type matchups can give you an advantage in battles.


Players strategize by considering the type matchups, Pokémon levels, move sets, and other factors. They decide which move to use, whether to switch Pokémon, or use items like healing potions.


Pokémon have Hit Points (HP), which represent their health. When a Pokémon's HP reaches zero, it faints and can no longer battle. Moves can also inflict status conditions like poisoning, paralysis, or sleep, affecting a Pokémon's performance.


Pokémon gain experience points (XP) when they participate in battles. Accumulating enough XP allows them to level up, increasing their stats and sometimes allowing them to learn new moves or evolve into more powerful forms.


The battle continues until one player defeats all of the opposing player's Pokémon. The winning player receives rewards such as money, items, and sometimes the defeated Pokémon may also provide the option to be caught.
